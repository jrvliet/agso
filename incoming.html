Recommended Readings

Your first two years will be filled with classes that will expand on your knowledge of astronomy. This works best if you have a knowledge of astronomy, which you have most likely gained from your undergraduate career or outside sources. The classes assume a base knowledge of astronomy. A good way of ensuring you're prepared is by reading up on the basics. Some recommended readings are:

  • Chapters from Carroll and Ostile , or BOB (Big Orange Book) as it is affectionatly called.
  • Chapter One from Rybicki and Lightman . This just about everything you need to know about radiative transfer, a topic covered in just about half of the courses you will take at NMSU.

Coding Skills

A very large part of research in astronomy is writing, reading, and using code. It is very difficult to succeed in astronomy without a strong background in coding. Several courses have large coding projects as part of their assignment, most notably ASTR506, where you will write a parallelized N-body code (this project is so big, the class is colloquially called "Nbody"), and ASTR545, where you will model a stellar atmosphere.

In addition to this, all classes will ask you to write small codes to read files, perform simple calculations, and plot results. There are many languages to pick from, you will need to fill four requirements:

  • Low-Level Language: These languages excel at fast and efficient computations (ie Nbody). There are two main choices for this: C or FORTRAN. Pick your favorite and master it!
  • High-Level Language: These are easy to write and use and are your go-to language for everyday tasks. Top picks are Matlab, IDL, and Perl. But by far the reigning champ around the department is Python.
  • Plotting Language: These do just what you think: plot data. The most popular choices are IDL and Python.
  • Writing Language: These are used for writing reports, papers, and just about anything. There is really only one choice: LaTeX. Regardless of how you pronounce it, it is the industry standard, and you will use it for everything you write.

To be prepared for the courses, pick language such as Python and learn how to write short scripts and run them. A major goal should be to read in a table of data and plot the results.

If you are looking for resources to help with learning a language, check out:

  • Code Academy
  • GitHub Resopistory of online computer classes from various universities.
  • Google's Python Course
