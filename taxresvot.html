Housing

    Students rent apartments or houses all over Las Cruces and Mesilla, and a few students have bought houses. Some of these were found via craigslist or Apartment Guide, but others were found by driving around town and investigating 'for rent' or 'for sale' signs. Not all apartment complexes have websites, but many are listed with contact information on googlemaps. The student government, ASNMSU, provides a free handbook to educate you about your options, recourses, and responsiblities as a renter. You can pick one up at the ASNMSU office (second floor of Corbett). You should also read up about renter's insurance. Where have people lived? Here's a quick summary:

    Casa Bandera - across the street from NMSU, swimming pool, small workout room, laundry in apartment, refrigerated air ... some haven't liked the undergraduate feel to it but others are perfectly fine with it
    the Grove - a block from NMSU, swimming pool ... some haven't liked the undergraduate feel to it but others are perfectly fine with it
    Las Palmas - across the street from NMSU, smaller but quiet, refrigerated air, laundry on site, allows small pets
    Mathers Realty owns many properties in Las Cruces, and one of them is across the street from NMSU at 1201 E. University Ave, evaporative cooling
    Villa Sierra Apartments - large apartment complex on Treviz (down the street from NMSU), refrigerated air, swimming pool, small workout room
    SunRidge Village - large apartment complex on Treviz (down the street from NMSU), swimming pool
    Tuscany Villa - large apartment complex on Roadrunner (some distance from NMSU), swimming pool
    Copperstone - large gated apartment complex on Roadrunner (some distance from NMSU), refrigerated air, laundry in apartment, swimming pool, allows pets
    Willow Brook Apartment Homes - large apartment complex past Rt. 70 (some distance from NMSU)

Driving in New Mexico

    Thankfully, you can look online for the New Mexico Motor Vehicle Division forms to get your driver's license. You may apply for a 4 or 8 year license. If you are under 25 years old and getting an NM license for the first time, you have to go through a drunk driving awareness program to get your license even if you hold a license in another state. The process takes about two weeks (plus any additional weeks due to inherent bureaucratic silliness).

    You may have noticed that the astro department has two cars of its own. In order to drive any vehicle from the NMSU fleet, including the department cars, you must have a permit from the university. To get the permit you have to register for and attend NMSU's 4 hour class on defensive driving. This class is only offered at certain times during the year and can fill up quickly, so register far in advance of actually needing the permit. There is a $40 enrollment fee, which the astro department with reimburse after you complete the course.

Filing State Taxes

    You can file online or download the forms and mail them to Sante Fe. Don't forget about your federal taxes! Some students have used TurboTax to file online for free.

Registering to Vote

    Participate in your government! Have a hand in shaping national policies! Have a reason to complain about who wins! Register to vote in New Mexico by filling out the Voter Registration Form and returning it to the ASNMSU office (Corbett Student Center) or they can deliver them directly to the Dona Ana County Bureau of Elections (845 N. Motel Blvd., Las Cruces, NM 88007, (505) 525-6156)). Forms are available from the following locations:

    Motor Vehicle Department (top pick!)
    ASNMSU Office
    Campus Activities
    Chicano Programs
    Black Programs
    American Indian Programs
    Center for Learning Assistance
    Student Health Center
    Housing Office
    Placement Office
    Registrar's Office
    Financial Aid Office
    Admissions Office
    Dona Ana County Bureau of Elections

    Also, voter registration information, including the New Mexico Voter Bill of Rights (English and Spanish versions) and links to the Native American Election Information Program, can be found at the Secretary of State's website. Finally, appropriate forms and instructions for registering to vote in each of the fifty states can be found at the Election Assistance website.
