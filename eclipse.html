Partial Solar Eclipse 2014

What Safely view a solar eclipse
When Thursday, October 23 3:00pm-6:00pm
Where Mesilla Valley Mall parking lot (Southwest corner)

On Thursday, October 23, the Moon will partially block the Sun in a solar eclipse from 3:34pm to 5:49pm (with a maximum eclipse at 4:45pm). The partial eclipse will only be visible from certain places on the planet, including New Mexico. The next solar eclipse visible from NM won't be until 2017.

The Moon will block 42% of the Sun's light in an annular eclipse, but it still won't be safe to stare at the Sun. The New Mexico State University Astronomy department, the Astronomical Society of Las Cruces, and the Las Cruces Natural History Museum would like you to join us in a safe solar watching event at the Mesilla Valley Mall (in the Southwest parking lot). Come and use our special solar telescopes and other safe Sun viewing equipment to see this spectacular heavenly tango!

In a solar eclipse the Moon will be directly between the Earth and the Sun. As the Moon goes around the Earth, it will cast its shadow on the Earth. The animation below illustrates the path of the Moon's shadow will take across the Earth on October 23.

If you can't attend the eclipse events, we encourage you to use the following methods to view the Sun safely:

Eclipse Glasses

Image courtesy Online Science Mall
Pinhole Projector
Welder's Glass (#14)

Image courtesy StarWatch
The NMSU Astronomy department is selling solar glasses for $1 each (or four for $3). Buy these ahead of time to share with your friends and family. Poke a small hole in a piece of paper and hold it up to the Sun. Let the light from the pinhole shine on another piece of paper and you'll see an image of the Sun. Welder's glass #14 is thick enough to view the Sun safely. Do not try this with any other kind of glass: sunglasses, tinted glass, and welder's glass #1-13 do not block enough light to provide safe viewing.
